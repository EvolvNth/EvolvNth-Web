Travel Planner

Notion · 3 databases · Trip planning made effortless

The best trips aren't spontaneous. They're designed so well that they feel effortless.

Built For

Weekend warriors, international adventurers, family trip planners, digital nomads, and anyone who's ever come home from a trip thinking "I wish I'd planned that better." Whether it's a 3-day city break or a 30-day backpacking route, this handles it.

The Problem

Your trip research lives in 20 browser tabs, a scattered Notes doc, and a text thread you can't find. You overspend because you never set a budget. You miss must-see spots because your itinerary was a vague list of "maybe" locations. And packing? You always forget something.

What's Inside

  • Trip Database — organize every trip with dates, destination, budget, status, and linked itineraries
  • Day-by-Day Itinerary Builder — plan each day with time blocks, activities, locations, estimated costs, and notes
  • Travel Budget Tracker — set a trip budget and track spending by category (flights, accommodation, food, activities)
  • Bucket List — lifetime destination list with priority ranking, estimated costs, and best travel seasons
  • Packing List Templates — reusable lists by trip type (beach, city, hiking, business) so you never forget essentials
  • Booking & Confirmation Hub — store flight numbers, hotel confirmations, and reservation details in one place

Two Ways to Use It

Beginner Mode

Pick your next trip, use the packing list template, and fill in the day-by-day itinerary with your must-do activities. The template keeps everything organized without overwhelming you.

Advanced Mode

Build multi-city routing plans, track spending against budget in real time, maintain a scored bucket list with seasonal optimization, and create reusable trip templates for repeat destinations.

The Science Behind It

Leverages the peak-end rule from Nobel laureate Daniel Kahneman's research: your memory of a trip is shaped by its peak moment and its ending. The itinerary builder helps you design those peaks deliberately rather than leaving them to chance.

Pre-trip planning reduces decision fatigue — the cognitive drain of making choices on the fly that turns a vacation into work. When logistics are handled in advance, you're free to be present and enjoy.

This is Nth-order design in practice: intentional planning (first order) creates richer, more immersive experiences (second order), which become core memories that strengthen relationships and motivation (third order), which compounds into a life defined by adventure rather than routine.

Why Others Fall Short

TripIt and travel apps organize confirmations but don't help you design the trip. Google Docs and Sheets are blank canvases with no structure. This template gives you reusable frameworks for every dimension of travel.

  • Travel apps handle bookings but ignore itinerary design, budgeting, and packing — the parts that make or break a trip
  • Generic Notion templates give you a single table and call it a planner — no budget tracking, no packing lists, no booking hub
  • Built with Nth-order design: other planners help you organize a trip. This one is engineered so each trip cascades into richer experiences, deeper relationships, and a life built around intentional adventure
