Content Creator Hub

Notion · 3 databases · Multi-platform management

The creators who win aren't more talented. They have a system that never lets a good idea die.

Built For

YouTubers, TikTokers, podcasters, bloggers, newsletter writers, and multi-platform creators at any stage — from your first 100 followers to established creators managing brand partnerships. If you create content on more than one platform, you need this.

The Problem

Your best ideas are scattered across Notes apps, voice memos, and screenshots you'll never find again. You post inconsistently because there's no pipeline. Brand deals slip through the cracks. And you spend more time managing the chaos than actually creating.

What's Inside

  • Content Pipeline Database — track every piece from idea → draft → production → published with status, platform, and publish date
  • Ideas Bank — capture ideas instantly with tags, priority scores, and platform fit so nothing gets lost
  • Brand Deals Tracker — manage sponsorships with deliverables, payment status, deadlines, and contact info
  • Multi-Platform Views — filtered views for YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, blog, podcast, and newsletter
  • Analytics Tracking — log performance metrics to identify what resonates and double down
  • Content Calendar — visual publishing schedule across all platforms

Two Ways to Use It

Beginner Mode

Start by dumping all your ideas into the Ideas Bank, then move your next 5 pieces into the pipeline. Follow the weekly content planning routine to build your first consistent publishing schedule.

Advanced Mode

Build content flywheels across platforms, track ROI on brand deals, analyze performance trends, implement batch production workflows, and run quarterly content audits to refine your strategy.

The Science Behind It

Built on the Content Flywheel concept — every piece feeds the next. Batching research from productivity science shows that grouping similar tasks reduces context-switching costs by up to 40%. The Ideas Bank applies the GTD capture habit from David Allen's Getting Things Done.

This is Nth-order thinking in action: one piece of content repurposed across platforms builds cumulative authority (first order), which attracts brand deals (second order), which funds better production (third order), which grows your audience and compounds your reach over time.

Why Others Fall Short

Buffer and Hootsuite handle publishing but not the creative process. Trello and Asana don't understand content workflows. This covers the entire lifecycle from idea to published post to brand deal payment.

  • Scheduling tools manage distribution but ignore ideation, production, and monetization
  • Generic project management apps require you to build a content workflow from scratch with no creator-specific structure
  • Built with Nth-order design: other tools help you post content. This one is engineered so consistent publishing compounds into audience growth, brand partnerships, and a sustainable creator business
